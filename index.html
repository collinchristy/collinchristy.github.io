Astrophysicist · Transient Phenomena

Collin Christy

I am an astrophysicist studying energetic transients across the universe using radio, millimeter, and X-ray telescopes including ALMA, VLA, Chandra, and Swift.

Explore research View publications

Current focus

Transient follow-up with an emphasis on compact object accretion, jet launching, and shock interaction in distant galaxies.

Approach

Broadband observations, data-driven modeling, and collaboration with multi-instrument teams to measure energetics and environments.

Impact

Translating transient physics into a clearer picture of how extreme cosmic explosions shape their host systems.

Research themes

I combine observations and theory across three major classes of energetic extragalactic transients.

Tidal disruption events

Probing black hole accretion and relativistic outflows produced when stars are torn apart in galactic nuclei.

Gamma-ray bursts

Studying jet physics and afterglow emission from the most powerful stellar explosions in the universe.

Engine-driven supernovae

Exploring unusual explosions powered by compact remnants and their signatures at radio and X-ray wavelengths.

Project gallery
